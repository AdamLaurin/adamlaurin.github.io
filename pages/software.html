Software and Game Development

In 2023, I made the decision to continue my education into a new direction which I had previously never considered. My interests in math and logic led me to study Computer Science at John Abbott College, where I completed my DEC in 2026.

My hope had always been to find some way to marry my experience in the humanities with software development. As I learned about data structures, database management, algorithms, and even full-stack web development, I had a constant eye aimed at questions related to how these could be helpful in both research and education in the humanities. But beyond this I also came to appreciate all of these fields in and of themselves and find excitement in development projects of all kinds.

Most recently, I have also developed a great interest in game development. I have always loved playing video games and when I made my first real game as a final project for one of my computer science courses, I realised just how much I enjoyed making them as well. This was reinforced when I did my internship with the EduQlasse lab at the Université de Montréal during my final semester, which builds educational video games for teachers who can use them in their courses.

Dados Toledanos

This project is a game inspired by real, historical dice games described in the 13th century Spanish work by king Alfonso X of Castile, the Libro de los juegos (Book of Games) or Libro de axedrez, dados e tablas (Book of Chess, Dice, and Tables).
Thus far I have recreated 3 of the games mentioned: Triga, Panquist, and Riffa.

This was the first game which I made myself, and it was also my first real project where I got to use programming to get a deeper look at lived history. Plus, it's actually quite fun!

This project was originally created as my final project for a game programming course in Fall 2025. It was made entirely in JavaScript rather than using an existing game engine, so that we could learn the processes and concepts behind game programming (though I did make use of matter.js for the dice physics). See this GitHub repo for the full code, as well as appropriate credits.

Project Hospitium (under development)

"Project Hospitium" is code name for a game I am currently working on which is still in its early stages. It is a narrative-focused 2D adventure game, inspired by the likes of Pentiment, Night in the Woods, and Life is Strange.

The game follows a teenaged orphan, Elise, who lives in a hospital in 13th century Tours, France. When she makes friends with the daughter of a burgher whose father sends her to the hospital to assist in charitable work, the two girls go on adventures together which lead to them accidentally uncovering corruption and scandal.

The game is being made in the Godot engine and will use 2D art inspired by the art of 13th century manuscript illuminations. Gameplay revolves around exploration, dialogue with various characters, and finding the right items to complete tasks.
