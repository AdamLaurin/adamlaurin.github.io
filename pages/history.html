History and the Humanities

I completed my MA in Medieval Studies at the University of Toronto in 2022. There my area of focus was especially early medieval religion and views about magic and miracles.

Since then, I have been creating online educational content to help bring good, academic information about history to popular audiences. This has mainly been in the form of my YouTube channel, Studium Historiae. However, I have also tried to find opportunities to teach in other contexts as well, such as when I got to assist in a historical and archeological tour of Spain in 2024, where I had the chance to give several lectures to students from John Abbott College and the University of Ottawa.

I have also been inspired by history in my more creative pursuits. You can see some of those on my Software and Game Development page.

Studium Historiae

Studium Historiae is a YouTube channel which I created in 2022 with the objective of bridging the gap between academic knowledge and perspectives on history and popular audiences. I had long noticed a great interest in historical topics by people on the internet, but the resources available have long been lacking. Viral posts and videos about history are often shallow or perpetuate woefully outdated ideas.

On the other hand, I had also seen a growing number of channels created by academics from various fields which had managed to gain large followings. These channels are able to fulfil the desires of curious individuals from various backgrounds who enjoy learning and want to understand topics beyond the surface level.

Since my first upload in October of 2022, I have been making new videos roughly every month with views in the thousands to hundreds of thousands per upload. With every video I am constantly trying to improve my presentation style and organization of topics in order for my videos to be approachable and interesting to a wide audience, while also continuing to provide high quality, well researched information.

The topics of my videos range from narrative surveys, like my video explaining the real history behind the Kingdom Come: Deliverance series, to conceptual deep dives, like my exploration of the idea of Convivencia in medieval Spain. The vast majority of my videos deal with medieval history, since that is the history with which I am the most familiar, but beyond that any topic is open. I like to embrace the multi-disciplinary nature of Medieval Studies by casting a wide net in terms of subjects and types of videos. In 2025 I even recorded my journey going on a medieval pilgrimage in France in an attempt to get a better feeling for what such an experience might have been like.
